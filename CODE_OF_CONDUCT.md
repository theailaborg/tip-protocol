# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

TIP Protocol exists to make the internet more trustworthy. We hold ourselves
to the same standard. Everyone who participates in this project: contributors,
maintainers, users, and community members: is expected to create a
welcoming, respectful, and productive environment.

## Standards

**Expected behaviour:**

- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully
- Focus on what is best for the protocol and the community
- Show empathy toward other community members

**Unacceptable behaviour:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Trolling, insulting, or personal attacks
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Sustained disruption of discussions or pull requests
- Any conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these
standards. They will take appropriate action in response to any behaviour
they deem inappropriate, which may include:

1. A written warning
2. Temporary ban from the repository and community spaces
3. Permanent ban

Reports of violations may be made to chairman@theailab.org. All reports
will be reviewed and investigated promptly. The identity of reporters
will be kept confidential.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces: GitHub issues, pull
requests, discussions, the community forum, and any other official channels.
