Key Security
How your private signing key is protected.
🔒 Your private key never leaves this browser.
It is encrypted with AES-256-GCM. The encryption key is derived from your device's biometric hardware (WebAuthn) or a password fallback. Only the encrypted ciphertext is stored. The raw key exists in memory only during signing.
Encryption architecture
1
Key derivation - WebAuthn (primary)
Path A (Chrome/Edge 116+): WebAuthn PRF extension derives a hardware-bound secret from the Secure Enclave via biometric authentication (Face ID / Touch ID). The AES key is SHAKE-256(PRF_output || domain). Hardware-bound - never leaves the chip.

Path B (Safari, Firefox, older Chrome): WebAuthn biometric gate + SHAKE-256(credentialId || domain) + PBKDF2 (200,000 iterations, random salt). Software-gated by biometrics.

Path C (fallback): Password + SHAKE-256(password || domain) + PBKDF2 (200,000 iterations). Standard password protection.
2
Encryption (AES-256-GCM)
Your ML-DSA-65 private key is encrypted with AES-256-GCM using a random 12-byte IV. AAD (Additional Authenticated Data) is bound to your TIP-ID - the ciphertext cannot be used with a different identity. The authentication tag detects any tampering.
3
Signing (ML-DSA-65, FIPS 204)
When you register content, the extension authenticates with your biometric (Face ID / Touch ID), decrypts the key in memory, signs the canonical JSON body (author_tip_id + origin_code + content_hash), then immediately wipes the key. The private key exists in plaintext memory for less than 100 milliseconds.
4
Network: only the signature is sent
The TIP node receives: author_tip_id, origin_code, content, and ML-DSA-65 signature. Your private key, biometric data, and encryption details are never transmitted. The node verifies the signature against your public key stored on the DAG.
Post-quantum cryptography: ML-DSA-65 (Dilithium3)
This extension uses ML-DSA-65 (FIPS 204) for all signatures - a post-quantum lattice-based scheme standardized by NIST. Secure against both classical and quantum computer attacks. Implemented via @noble/post-quantum bundled directly into the extension.

When a platform adds native TIP support
Once a platform publishes TIP-Author, TIP-Content, and TIP-Trust-Score HTTP headers on their responses, the extension automatically switches to reading those instead. How It Works
End-to-end guide for creators.
For Creators: Registering Content
1
Get a TIP-ID
Apply for verification at vp.theailab.org/get-verified. A Verification Provider (VP) runs you through biometric verification - government ID, 3D liveness check, device binding. Takes about 10 minutes. You receive your TIP-ID and private key.
2
Connect to the browser extension
After verification, click "Connect Browser Plugin" on the VP page. Your encrypted signing key is transferred to the extension via secure messaging. The key is encrypted with your device biometric (Face ID / Touch ID) - it can only be decrypted by you on this device.
3
Go to your upload page
Navigate to YouTube Studio, TikTok upload, Instagram create, or any supported platform. The TIP panel appears automatically in the bottom-right corner of the page.
4
Select your origin code
Choose how this content was created:
OH - Original Human: you created it without AI.
AA - AI-Assisted: you led the work, AI helped.
AG - AI-Generated: AI generated it.
MX - Mixed: combination of sources.

Over-declaring AI involvement is always safe. Declaring OH when AI generated it can cost trust score points.
5
Authenticate and register
Tap Face ID or Touch ID when prompted. The extension decrypts your signing key, hashes your content, signs it with ML-DSA-65, and sends the registration to the TIP node. Your key is wiped from memory immediately after signing. Takes under 2 seconds.
6
Copy your CTID and paste it
You receive a CTID: tip://c/OH-7f2a91bc3d5e4a-a3f8. Paste it in your video description, post caption, or article. Viewers with the TIP extension installed see your verified badge. Anyone can verify it at vp.theailab.org/verify-record.
For Viewers: What the Badges Mean
🟢
Highly Trusted (800–1000)
Long track record of accurate origin declarations, no disputes, consistent honest labelling. The most trustworthy tier on the network.
🔵
Trusted (600–799)
Verified identity, clean history, reliable origin declarations. The standard tier for credible creators.
🟡
Review Advised (400–599)
New identity or some disputes on record. Content may be accurate but verify independently for high-stakes topics.
🔴
Not Trusted (0–199)
Multiple confirmed origin mislabelling offenses. Treat content with significant caution.
