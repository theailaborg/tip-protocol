TIP Protocol

How scoring works

A plain-English guide for users and contributors.

The basics

  • Every new identity starts at 500 points.
  • Score range: 0 to 1000.
  • Your score is computed deterministically from every transaction touching your TIP-ID — every node in the network arrives at the same number from the same history.

What earns you points

As a creator

  • Each verification on your content: +2 to +5
  • Your content is cleared by a dispute (vindication): +5
  • 90-day clean record bonus: +10

As a reviewer / juror / expert

  • Reviewer makes a correct call: +5
  • Juror votes with the majority (Stage 2): +3
  • Expert votes with the majority (Stage 3 appeal): +7
  • Disputer or appellant wins their case: stake refunded + bonus (see below)

What costs you points

As a creator

  • Voluntarily retract your content: -50
  • Accept a reviewer's correction privately: -10
  • Lose a dispute (mislabeling confirmed): see mislabeling table
  • Lose an appeal you filed: appeal stake stays forfeited

As a participant

  • File a dispute: -15 held as stake; refunded only if you win
  • File an appeal: -25 held as stake; refunded only if you win
  • Juror votes against the majority (Stage 2): -8
  • Expert votes against the majority (Stage 3): -10
  • Summoned but never committed (no engagement): -1 — same for juror or expert
  • Juror committed but missed the reveal: -8
  • Expert committed but missed the reveal: -10

Mislabeling penalties

If you declare your content's origin incorrectly and a dispute confirms the mislabeling, the penalty depends on how serious the misdeclaration was and how many prior offenses you have.

You declared It actually was 1st offense 2nd 3rd+
Original Human (OH)AI-Generated (AG)-100-200-300
Original Human (OH)AI-Assisted (AA)-40-80-120
AI-Assisted (AA)AI-Generated (AG)-25-50-75
Over-labeling (e.g. AA→OH, AG→OH)0 — no penalty, encouraged
Over-declaring AI involvement is always free. Saying "AI-Assisted" when it was actually all-human costs nothing — we want creators to default toward caution. Penalties only apply when you under-declare and the call gets confirmed by a jury.

Walk-throughs

You publish OH content, someone disputes it, jury sides with you

Outcome: dispute DISMISSED.

  • Your score: +5 (vindication bonus).
  • Disputer's score: -15 (filing stake stays forfeited — they were wrong).

You publish OH content, someone disputes it, jury upholds the dispute

First offense, declared OH but actually AI-Assisted.

  • Your score: -40 (1st-offense OH→AA penalty).
  • Your content's origin label gets corrected to AA.
  • Disputer's score: +20 (stake refund + UPHELD_BONUS).
  • Jurors who voted MISMATCH: +3 each.

You appeal a dispute you lost, and the experts overturn it

  • Stage-2 -40 penalty reversed: +40.
  • Appeal stake refunded + bonus: +35.
  • Vindication bonus: +5.
  • You paid -25 to file the appeal, so net from the appeal alone: +15. Plus the -40 reversal = your score returns to where it was before the original dispute, plus +15 in bonuses.

Why these numbers?

  • Honest creators stay safe. Over-labeling is free. Penalties only fire on confirmed under-declaration.
  • Mislabeling is expensive. A first offense for OH-as-AG (the worst kind) costs -100. Repeat offenses scale up.
  • Frivolous disputes are discouraged. The -15 stake means you only file when you actually believe the content is mislabeled.
  • Reviewers and jurors are rewarded for good work, not punished for trying. No upfront stake — only verdict-time settlement (juror +3 / -8 minority, expert +7 / -10 minority). A juror or expert who never engages at all takes only -1; the heavier penalty (-8 or -10) kicks in only when you commit then walk away.
  • Severity scaling is preserved across repeat offenses. A repeat AA→AG offender doesn't get the OH→AG penalty applied to them.

What's NOT scored (and why)

If you've seen our internal spec doc and wonder why certain things aren't here — these are deliberate choices we made during implementation:

  • No stake from jurors, experts, or reviewers. The spec said each role should stake points up front, refunded if their decision held up. We removed that. They earn at verdict time (+3 juror majority / +7 expert majority / +5 reviewer correct call) or lose: -8 juror or -10 expert on minority, -1 for never committing, and -8 juror or -10 expert for committing-then-missing-reveal. Cleaner, same final economics shape, no in-flight escrow to track. Disputers and appellants still stake — that's anti-spam pressure on filing, a different problem.
  • No milestone bonuses. "+15 at 10 reviews, +30 at 50, badges at 200" — we decided reputation already accumulates naturally from correct decisions. No separate streak rewards.
  • No retroactive DISMISS penalty. If a reviewer dismisses a case and a separate later dispute proves it was actually mislabeling, we don't go back and dock them. The eligibility gate (overturn rate ≤ 30%) handles consistently-bad reviewers automatically.
  • No 90-day clean-record reset (yet). Your offense count doesn't decay to zero after 90 clean days — it stays. We're holding on this until we see real offense-distribution data in production.
  • Community Verifier scoring (clicking the badge to vouch for content) — not built yet. Possibly in scope later; not committed.
